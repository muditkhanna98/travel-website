GETTING AROUND JAPAN

Japan is an island nation that is easy both to reach, and to navigate once you get there. The country’s transportation infrastructure is sophisticated and extensive, so no matter where you go, you will find it easy to travel on to your next destination.

PUBLIC TRANSPORT

SHINKANSEN BULLET TRAIN

The Shinkansen is Japan's best-known train, with front that resembles a space rocket, and are the most convenient way of discovering Japan. The Rail network is extensive and reach a top speed of 320 km/h.

DOMESTIC FLIGHTS

Because of the long distances between, for example, Fukuoka on the island of Kyushu and Sapporo on Hokkaido, flying is often the fastest way to travel, with airlines like JAL and ANA providing the most extensive coverage. Smaller domestic airlines with more competitive rates include Skymark, Solaseed Air and Jetstar Japan.

FERRIES

Ferries are a major form of transportation to Japan’s many islands. Smaller islands, like Naoshima in the island-studded Seto Inland Sea, can only be reached by ferry. Long-distance ferries may provide a more relaxing and cheaper alternative to planes.

MONORAIL

As well as the subway, Japan has an extensive monorail system, and one Japanese line is the second longest monorail in the world. Big cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Hiroshima all operate monorails.Riding the monorail in Tokyo is a memorable experience, because you can often enjoy seaside views.
